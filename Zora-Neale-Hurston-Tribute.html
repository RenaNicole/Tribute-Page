Zora Neale Hurston

Writer, folklorist, and anthropologist, Hurston was called Queen of The Harlem Renaissance and "a Genius of the South."

Photo of the author herself.

Significant moments in Zora's Life:

  • 1891 - Born in Notasuolga, Alabama and moved with her family to Eatonville, Florida when she was a toddler.
  • 1918 -Enrolled at historically black college Howard University in Washington, D.C.
  • 1925 - First moved to New York City, shortly after was offered a scholarship to attend the all women's Barnard College, where she was the first and sole black student.
  • 1926 - Hustron along with Langston Huges and Wallace Thurman, produced a literary magazine called Fire!!! that featured other young artists during the Harlem Renaissance.
  • 1928 - Received her B.A. in anthroplology at age 37.
  • 1929 - Moved to Eau Gallie, Florida where she wrote Mules and Men one of Huston's most significant works focused on African-American folklore, later published in 1935.
  • 1936-1937- Traveled to Haiti and Jamaica to research folk traditions for her anthropological work, Tell My Horse (1938).
  • 1937 - Published Their Eyes Were Watching God a classic and one of Hurston's best known work.
  • 1939 - Published Moses, Man of the Mountain
  • 1960 - Passes at age 69
